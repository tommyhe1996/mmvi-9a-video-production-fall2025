## 4) Zoom H4n — Fast Start
- Power on (hold side switch ~3s). Choose input: built‑in mics (`MIC`), XLR (`1`/`2`), or 1/8" jack. fileciteturn0file0  
- Built‑in mic **pattern**: **90°** (narrow) vs **120°** (wide). Use windscreens whenever possible. fileciteturn0file0  
- **Levels:** Press `REC` once (standby), set gain; target ≈ **–12 dB** avg for dialogue. fileciteturn0file1  
- **Record:** Press `REC` twice to roll; `STOP` to end; `PLAY` to review. fileciteturn0file0

> **Pro tip:** Always bring fresh AAs and clear/backup your SD card before shoots. fileciteturn0file0

## 5) Boom Technique Essentials
- Keep the mic **on‑axis** to the mouth; hold steady distance; move quietly; remove rings that can tap the pole. fileciteturn0file0  
- Coordinate with camera for **frame lines** and watch for **boom shadows**. Rehearse positions. fileciteturn0file0  
- Between takes, **rest arms** and reset. Booming is physical—plan handoffs with the recorder holder. fileciteturn0file0

---

## 6) Recording Quality: Key Concepts
- **Signal‑to‑Noise Ratio:** Get the mic close; reduce ambient noise; choose quieter spaces. fileciteturn0file1  
- **Recording Levels:** Avoid “hot” (clipping) or too low (hiss). Aim for **–12 dB** nominal. fileciteturn0file1  
- **Room Tone:** Capture **≥30 seconds** per setup to glue edits and maintain ambience. fileciteturn0file1  
- **Terminology:** Production sound, single vs. double system, MOS, ambient, wild sound, sample rate, bit depth. fileciteturn0file0

## 8) Lab B — Interview Fundamentals
**Frame & Eyeline**
- Place subject screen **left or right** (rule of thirds); keep eyeline **near lens** to build viewer connection. fileciteturn0file2  
- Capture **two compositions** (e.g., **MCU** then **CU**) to help with editing. fileciteturn0file2

**Sound & Space**
- Shut windows/doors; kill noise sources; place Zoom/boom **as close as possible** (out of frame). fileciteturn0file2

**Question Prompts (open‑ended)**
- Introduce yourself; your BCC experience; growth as a filmmaker; career aspirations; would you recommend BCC? fileciteturn0file2
