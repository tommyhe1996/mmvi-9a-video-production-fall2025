## üé¨ Lecture Summary: The Power of Sound in Storytelling
Sound helps guide emotional tone, suspense, and audience connection.

### Key Concepts
- **Sound influences emotion faster than visuals.** The brain processes sound more rapidly, shaping how we interpret scenes.  
- **Sound design begins at conception.** Consider sound cues when planning your story.  
- **Diegetic Sound:** Heard by characters (dialogue, footsteps, ambient sounds).  
- **Non-Diegetic Sound:** Heard only by the audience (score, narration, sound effects).  
:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

### Sound Production & Post Terms
| Term | Description |
|------|--------------|
| **Score** | Music that contributes to tone and mood. |
| **Foley** | Studio-recorded everyday sounds (footsteps, clothing). |
| **ADR (Automated Dialogue Replacement)** | Re-recording dialogue in studio for clarity or creative intent. |
| **Sound Design** | The full creative process combining score, effects, and mixing. |
| **Signal-to-Noise Ratio** | Balance between desired sound (voice) and background noise. |

## üé§ Zoom Recorder & Audio Kit Setup

### Equipment List
- **Zoom H4n Recorder** (with SD card and AA batteries)  
- **Shotgun Mic** + Shock Mount + Boom Pole  
- **Headphones** (wired only, no Bluetooth)  
- **Y-Splitter, XLR cable, and foam windscreen ("dead cat")**

### Recorder Setup Steps
1. Insert batteries & SD card.  
2. **Turn off ‚ÄúStamina Mode.‚Äù**  
3. **Factory reset** and **format SD card.**  
4. Set **recording format:** WAV, 48 kHz, 24-bit.  
5. Turn **Phantom Power ON (24V)** for mics that need power.  
6. Choose **Stereo Mode.**  
7. Connect the mic and headphones.  
8. Test-record and monitor levels between **‚Äì6 dB and ‚Äì12 dB.**  
9. Back up or delete old recordings.

## üéôÔ∏è Boom Operation & Technique

### Best Practices
- Maintain a **consistent distance** between mic and speaker‚Äôs mouth.  
- Avoid casting **boom shadows** or entering the frame.  
- Use your **body and fingertips** to smoothly adjust mic angle.  
- Work closely with camera operators for frame lines.  
- **Monitor with headphones** at all times.  
- Remove jewelry or anything that might tap the pole.  

> üí™ Tip: Boom operating is physically demanding‚Äîdevelop upper-body strength for steadier operation.

## üéß Recording Levels and Room Tone
- **Dialogue levels:** around **‚Äì12 dB.**  
- **Too high:** distorted; **too low:** hiss.  
- Always record **room tone (30 seconds minimum)** at the end of every take.  
  - Captures natural ambience to ‚Äúglue‚Äù edited dialogue together.

## üßµ Cable Management: Over-Under Method
To extend cable life and prevent tangling:
1. Loop first coil normally (over).  
2. Flip wrist for the second coil (under).  
3. Alternate ‚Äúover‚Äìunder‚Äù pattern until finished.  
4. Secure with velcro or tie line.

## üé§ Framing & Recording Interviews
### Composition
- Place subject **screen left or right** following the **Rule of Thirds.**  
- Maintain eyeline near the camera lens for emotional connection.  
- Include environmental context to enhance storytelling.  
:contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

### Conducting the Interview
- Write **open-ended questions** beforehand.  
- Advise subjects **not to wear stripes or busy patterns.**  
- Record in quiet space ‚Äî close doors/windows.  
- Use **two framing compositions:** MCU and CU.  
- Place **Zoom recorder or boom mic** close to the subject.  

### Sample Lab Exercise
Interview a classmate:
1. Frame shot MCU.  
2. Ask to **state name or count to ten** to check levels.  
3. Ask:
   - Introduce yourself.  
   - How has your filmmaking experience evolved?  
   - What are your career aspirations?  
   - Would you recommend BCC to others?
